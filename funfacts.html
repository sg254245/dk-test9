Fun Facts and Memes


The Super Mario Bros. Movie

Link to source: Super Mario Wiki

In The Super Mario Bros. Movie, Donkey Kong is introduced in the Great Ring of Kong to the chorus portion of the DK Rap, which chants his name.

Prior to the film's release, a teaser for the film posted on social media confirmed the inclusion of the DK Rap in the film. In the teaser, Donkey Kong's film voice actor Seth Rogen states that the song is "objectively one of the worst rap songs of all time."

Songwriter Grant Kirkhope expressed joy on the X networking platform upon learning about the song's inclusion in the film, but was later disappointed in seeing he was not credited as the original composer in the movie's credits.

Lankyposting

Link to source: Know Your Meme

Lankyposting, also known as HE, refers to the practice of posting images of Lanky Kong, a character from the 1999 video game Donkey Kong 64. The meme originated as a satire of fan enthusiasm for including certain characters in the Super Smash Bros. roster. Fans humorously campaigned for Lanky Kong's inclusion in the game, despite his low relevance to the series.

On August 14th, 2014, a 4chan user first coined the term "Lankyposting" in a thread discussing video game characters. Soon after, on September 6th, Tumblr user nicocw posted a photoshopped image of Lanky Kong in front of an inspirational quote background while reciting his verse in the DK Rap. This post gained over 4,500 notes in just three weeks.

Lankyposting has since evolved, with users sharing increasingly absurd and exaggerated tributes to Lanky Kong across various platforms like Reddit, Twitter, and YouTube. It remains a niche but persistent meme within gaming culture.
